<h2>Diet</h2>


<div style="text-align:left">

<div style="background-color:grey">

<div style="color:white">

<p>Koala bears love gumleaves. Gumleaves are toxic, fibrous, and have low-nutrition. If a Koala bear eats too many gumleaves, it will require a lot of energy to digest them, so sleeping is the best way to conserve energy. An adult Koala bear can eat about one half of a kilogram to 1 kilogram of leaves every night. This ultimately depends on the size, sex, and location of the koala bear. Koala bears rarely need to drink water because most of their hydration comes from the gumleaves.</p></div></div></div>


<h2 class="topic">Habitat</h2>


<div style="text-align:left">

<div style="background-color:grey">

<div style="color:white">

<p>Koala bears live in a range of different habitats within Australia. This range includes coastal islands, tall eucalypt forests, and low inland woodlands. The two most important factors when deciding where to reside include, the presence of trees preferred by Koalas and the number of other koalas that inhabit a given area. Unfortunately, about 80% of the Koala bear’s habitat has been obliterated, and almost none of the remaining 20% is protected.</p></div></div></div>


<h2 class="topic">Fun Facts</h2>


<div style="text-align:left">

<div style="background-color:grey">

<div style="color:white">

<p>Here are some interesting, fun facts that you may not have already known about Koala Bears.</p>


<ul style="list-style-type:circle">

  <li>Koala bears are actually not bears at all. They are Marsupials. This means that their young are born immature and further develop in a pouch.</li>

  <li>Infant koala bears are called a joey's because they live in their mother's pouch for about 6 months to continue growing</li>

  <li>Koala Bears get all of their needed moisture from the eucalyptus leaves that they eat. They have no need to drink water.</li>

  <li>Koala bears can sleep for up to 18 hours a day.</li>

</ul></div></div></div>
